Crispy Golden Samosa

Perfectly crispy triangular pastries filled with spiced potatoes and peas. A beloved teatime snack that brings joy to every bite.

Ingredients

For the Dough

  • 2 cups All-Purpose Flour (Maida)
  • 4 tbsp Ghee or Oil
  • 1/2 tsp Carom Seeds (Ajwain)
  • 1/2 tsp Salt
  • Water as needed (~1/2 cup)

For the Filling

  • 4 medium Potatoes, boiled & mashed
  • 1/2 cup Green Peas
  • 2 tbsp Oil
  • 1 tsp Cumin Seeds
  • 1 tsp Ginger, finely chopped
  • 2 Green Chilies, chopped
  • 1 tsp Coriander Powder
  • 1/2 tsp Garam Masala
  • 1/2 tsp Amchur (Dry Mango Powder)
  • 1/2 tsp Red Chili Powder
  • 1 tsp Fennel Seeds, crushed
  • 2 tbsp Fresh Coriander, chopped
  • Salt to taste
  • Oil for deep frying

Method

Step 1 of 20
1
Preparing the Dough

In a large mixing bowl, combine all-purpose flour, salt, and carom seeds. Mix well.

2
Preparing the Dough

Add ghee or oil to the flour mixture. Rub it into the flour with your fingers until the mixture resembles breadcrumbs.

3
Preparing the Dough

Gradually add water, a little at a time, and knead into a firm, smooth dough. The dough should not be too soft.

4
Preparing the Dough

Cover the dough with a damp cloth and let it rest for 30 minutes.

5
Preparing the Filling

Heat 2 tablespoons of oil in a pan over medium heat. Add cumin seeds and let them splutter.

6
Preparing the Filling

Add chopped ginger and green chilies. Saut√© for 30 seconds until fragrant.

7
Preparing the Filling

Add green peas and cook for 2-3 minutes until tender.

8
Preparing the Filling

Add the mashed potatoes, coriander powder, garam masala, amchur, red chili powder, fennel seeds, and salt. Mix well and cook for 3-4 minutes, stirring occasionally.

9
Preparing the Filling

Add chopped coriander leaves, mix well, and turn off the heat. Let the filling cool completely before using.

10
Assembling the Samosas

Divide the rested dough into 6-7 equal portions and roll them into balls.

11
Assembling the Samosas

Roll each ball into an oval shape (approximately 6-7 inches long). Cut each oval in half to create two semi-circles.

12
Assembling the Samosas

Take one semi-circle and apply water along the straight edge. Form a cone shape by bringing the two ends of the straight edge together, overlapping slightly, and pressing firmly to seal.

13
Assembling the Samosas

Fill the cone with 2-3 tablespoons of the potato filling, leaving some space at the top.

14
Assembling the Samosas

Apply water on the inner edges of the opening and press firmly to seal the samosa. You can crimp the edges with a fork for a decorative pattern and better sealing.

15

Repeat with the remaining dough and filling.

16

Heat oil in a deep pan or kadhai over medium heat. To test if the oil is ready, drop a small piece of dough‚Äîit should rise slowly to the surface.

17

Gently slide 3-4 samosas into the hot oil. Do not overcrowd the pan.

18
Frying the Samosas

Fry on medium-low heat for 12-15 minutes, turning occasionally, until the samosas are golden brown and crispy on all sides.

19
Frying the Samosas

Remove the samosas with a slotted spoon and drain on paper towels.

20

Serve hot with tamarind chutney, mint chutney, or tomato ketchup.

Pro Tips for Perfect Samosas

The key to crispy samosas is frying them on medium-low heat. High heat will brown them quickly but leave them undercooked inside.
Make sure the filling is completely cool before assembling to prevent the dough from becoming soggy.
Seal the edges properly to prevent the filling from leaking during frying.
You can freeze unfried samosas and fry them directly from frozen when needed.
For a healthier version, brush samosas with oil and bake at 180¬∞C (350¬∞F) for 30-35 minutes, turning halfway through.
